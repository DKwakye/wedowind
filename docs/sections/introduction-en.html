Introduction back to ToC

The wind energy sector faces a dual challenge: advancing digitalisation through data-driven methods while building workforce competencies to deploy these technologies effectively. Benchmarking competitions have emerged as a critical mechanism for comparing prognostics, forecasting, and fault detection approaches. However, the proliferation of challenges, datasets, and methods has created fragmentation—hindering reproducibility, cross-study comparison, and systematic skill development. The WeDoWind Ontology addresses this gap by providing a comprehensive semantic framework that unifies three interconnected domains: 1. Benchmarking & Evaluation Challenges decompose into tasks targeting specific failure modes (gearbox, bearing, blade) using defined datasets and turbines. Submissions implement computational methods evaluated by standardized metrics, with leaderboard entries capturing ranked performance. Crucially, the ontology distinguishes :usesDatasetForTask (official challenge data) from :usesDatasetForSubmission (actual data used, including external sources), ensuring transparent lineage and reproducibility. 2. Training & Competency Development Tutorials target specific competencies (SCADA analysis, machine learning prognosis) with defined difficulty levels and prerequisites. Training pathways sequence tutorials and challenges to scaffold learning. Challenge tasks :addressesCompetency while tutorials :targetsCompetency, enabling competency-based curriculum design for initiatives such as the Horizon Europe DC Network. 3. Interdisciplinary Research Integration Recognizing wind energy as a socio-technical domain, the ontology incorporates survey instruments for community acceptance studies, safety culture assessments, and workforce evaluations. Sensor variables and survey variables share a common :Variable superclass, enabling unified analysis across physical and social data. Built on persistent w3id.org identifiers and aligned with W3C standards, WeDoWind provides a foundation for the TWEED Knowledge Graph—supporting both the WeDoWind benchmarking platform and the Training Wind Energy Experts on Digitalisation network. By standardizing the representation of challenges, competencies, and interdisciplinary research, WeDoWind fosters reproducibility, cross-study comparison, and systematic skill development—accelerating the digital transformation of the wind energy sector.

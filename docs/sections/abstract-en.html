Abstract

The WeDoWind Ontology is a formal knowledge representation framework for wind energy benchmarking challenges, training, and interdisciplinary research. It provides a unified semantic vocabulary that bridges technical benchmarking—challenges, tasks, submissions, leaderboards, and computational methods—with training pathways, competencies, and educational resources. The ontology extends established standards (schema.org, DDI Lifecycle, SKOS, PROV-O) to model wind energy-specific entities including turbines, failure modes, SCADA sensor variables, and survey instruments. Key features include: (i) explicit dataset lineage distinguishing official task data from submission data sources; (ii) competency-based linking of challenges to tutorials and learning pathways; (iii) provenance-aware reference metadata for automated citation linking; and (iv) controlled vocabularies for methodological classification. By integrating physical asset modeling with social science research instruments, WeDoWind supports reproducible benchmarking, structured training curricula, and cross-disciplinary knowledge discovery for the wind energy digitalisation community.
